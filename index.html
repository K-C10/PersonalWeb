Cole Walser

The battlefield was a sugar-coated wasteland, torn apart by the clash between the sour rebels and the chocolate empire. The Sour Patch Kids, small but fierce, launched a tangy assault, their gummy bodies bouncing as they stormed the Hershey fortress. The air was thick with the scent of sugar and artificial fruit as the Sour Patch artillery rained down a barrage of powdered citric acid, dissolving the defenses of the chocolate stronghold. The Hershey Bars stood firm, their smooth chocolate armor melting under the relentless attacks, but their disciplined marshmallow reinforcements held the lines.

Caramel rivers flowed like molten lava, and cookie crumbles littered the ground as makeshift barricades. The chocolate knights, led by the formidable King Hershey, wielded candy cane lances and unleashed waves of molten fudge upon the invading sour forces. But the Sour Patch Kids were unpredictable—they struck with speed, first sour, then sweet, overwhelming their chocolate foes with relentless energy.

As the battle raged on, peanut butter reinforcements arrived, tipping the scales in Hershey’s f'avor. But just as the chocolate empire prepared to claim victory, a new force emerged from the depths of the candy aisle: the Gummy Bear mercenaries, fighting for their own gain. Chaos erupted as all sides clashed in a sticky, syrupy war. In the end, only one faction could rule the candy kingdom—but with so much sweetness turned to ruin, was it even worth it?
